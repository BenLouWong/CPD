Childrens Emergencies

Dental injuries and accidents can happen anywhere, anytime. It’s critical for parents to have basic knowledge about how to handle these serious situations. If a dental practice or dentist is not immediately available, refer to the below steps to minimise your child’s discomfort and prevent further damage.

For temporary relief, rinse the area around the sore tooth with warm salt water. Use dental floss to gently remove any food trapped between the teeth. Do not put aspirin on the gum or tooth. If the pain persists, call us immediately.

Rinse the mouth with water to temporarily relieve the pain, then call us immediately. Do not try to re-implant the tooth. Primary teeth should not be replanted as this process risks damaging the permanent successor.

If an adult tooth is knocked out, follow the below steps to ease the pain, then call us immediately: Handle the tooth by the top (crown), not the root portion. Try to reinsert it into the socket. If it cannot be done, keep the tooth in a cup of milk or wrap it in a plastic wrap and avoid touching when necessary. Once stored in milk the tooth should be reiplanted by a dentsit wihtin 30 minutes if possible.

A toothache that is severe and continuous and results in gnawing or throbbing pain or sharp or shooting pain are common symptoms of an abscessed tooth. Other symptoms may include: Fever, pain when chewing, sensitivity of the teeth to hot or cold, bitter taste in the mouth, foul smell to the breath (Halitosis), swollen neck glands, general discomfort, uneasiness, or ill feeling, redness and swelling of the gums (Gingivitis, Periodontitis), swollen area of the upper or lower jaw and an open, draining sore on the side of the gum

If your child has chipped or broken a piece off a tooth, try to locate the tooth fragment that broke off and call to make an appointment as soon as possible.

Broken braces can cause irritation and damage to the mouth. If your child’s braces have broken, call for an appointment. In the meantime, follow the below steps to ease your child’s pain:
Check the wires. If they are still connected to the bracket, use a cotton tip to gently push it back
Use orthodontic wax to cover the wire, so that it does not poke or irritate the flesh. You can alternatively use a cotton ball, gauze or chewing gumk
