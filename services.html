Preventive

Preventive dental procedures aim to minimise the chances of dental problems from an early age. This includes the likes of the following

  • Regular dental visits
  • Fissure Sealants

    Back teeth have fissures or grooves on their eating surfaces. Deep fissures traps plaque and food more easily thus increase chance of decay. By sealing these fissures, it reduces plaque trap and the risk of decay

  • Dental Radiographs

    Dental Radiographs are important to diagnose clinically invisible areas of tooth decay. Once diagnosed they can be treated earlier and often with conservative filling

  • Oral hygiene instructions (brushing, flossing, fluoride)
  • Dietary Advice
  • Mouth Guards

    • Restorative

    Restorative Dentistry aims to provide aesthetic improvements to your childs smile. This includes

  • Stainless Steel Crowns

    Stainless steel crowns are silver coloured “caps” used to restore teeth that are badly decayed or need a nerve treatment. Stainless steel crowns are the restorations of choice for molar teeth in children and is the recognised standard of care.

  • Adhesive Fillings
  • Dentures

    Emergency

    Management of dental trauma is important in both primary and permanent dentition. Correct assessment, diagnosis and long-term management can pave the way for a long-term functioning dentition. Paediatric dentists are trained in the management of all dentoalveolar injuries in children and adolescents. If a dental practice or dentist is not immediately available, refer to the below steps to minimise your child’s discomfort and prevent further damage.

    For temporary relief, rinse the area around the sore tooth with warm salt water. Use dental floss to gently remove any food trapped between the teeth. Do not put aspirin on the gum or tooth. If the pain persists, call us immediately.

    Rinse the mouth with water to temporarily relieve the pain, then call us immediately. Do not try to re-implant the tooth. Primary teeth should not be replanted as this process risks damaging the permanent successor.

    If an adult tooth is knocked out, follow the below steps to ease the pain, then call us immediately: Handle the tooth by the top (crown), not the root portion. Try to reinsert it into the socket. If it cannot be done, keep the tooth in a cup of milk or wrap it in a plastic wrap and avoid touching when necessary. Once stored in milk the tooth should be reiplanted by a dentsit wihtin 30 minutes if possible.

    A toothache that is severe and continuous and results in gnawing or throbbing pain or sharp or shooting pain are common symptoms of an abscessed tooth. Other symptoms may include: Fever, pain when chewing, sensitivity of the teeth to hot or cold, bitter taste in the mouth, foul smell to the breath (Halitosis), swollen neck glands, general discomfort, uneasiness, or ill feeling, redness and swelling of the gums (Gingivitis, Periodontitis), swollen area of the upper or lower jaw and an open, draining sore on the side of the gum

    If your child has chipped or broken a piece off a tooth, try to locate the tooth fragment that broke off and call to make an appointment as soon as possible.

    Broken braces can cause irritation and damage to the mouth. If your child’s braces have broken, call for an appointment. In the meantime, follow the below steps to ease your child’s pain:
    Check the wires. If they are still connected to the bracket, use a cotton tip to gently push it back
    Use orthodontic wax to cover the wire, so that it does not poke or irritate the flesh. You can alternatively use a cotton ball, gauze or chewing gum.

    Growth & Development and Orthodontics

    Paediatric dentists watch the growth and development of your child. They identify and intervene with habits and dental anomilies such as thumb sucking or the number of teeth. These habits can lead to aesthetic and functional problems in the adult dentition

    We offer the following treatments for paediatric dental needs as well as full fixed orthodontic needs:

    Removable Functional Appliances

  • Space Maintainers

    They are used to hold space from the early loss of a tooth so the adjacent teeth don’t move into that space

  • Thumb Sucking Plate

    Used for stopping thumb sucking habits

  • Twin Blocks

    Used for correcting jaw relationship during growth.

    • Treatment under sedation or general anaesthesia

    Some procedures require the use of sedation or general anaesthesia on your child to give them a pain free experience. In our procedures we use the following safe methods of sedation:

    Nitrous Oxide

    Nitrous oxide sedation is a safe and easy to use inhalational relaxant. In combination with a local anesthetic on an apprehensive but generally cooperative patient, it provides an excellent avenue to facilitate all aspects of dentistry in children.

    General Anaesthesia

    General anesthetics are a safe and sensible option for the treatment of patients with extensive dental disease and other medical conditions. These procedures are performed in a safe hospital setting with pediatric trained anesthetists. The procedures usually take no longer than 30-60 minutes and the children are discharged home on the same day.

    Children with Anxiety

    Paediatric Dentists are trained in the management of nervous and anxious children. An understanding of growth and the emotional needs of a child and adolescent are paramount in the treatment of anxious children.

    Children with Special Needs

    As hospital-based trained specialists, pediatric dentists are exposed to a great diversity of patients with special needs. These special needs include emotional, physical, intellectual, and congenital development needs. Pediatric dentists are aware of the needs of these patients and the requirement to have good dental health to complement their overall health.
